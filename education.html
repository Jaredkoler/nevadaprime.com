Understanding lymphedema and your treatment options

What is Lymphedema?

Lymphedema is a chronic condition that causes swelling ‚Äî most commonly in the arms or legs ‚Äî due to a compromised lymphatic system. The lymphatic system is responsible for draining excess fluid and waste from your tissues. When it isn't working properly, fluid accumulates and causes persistent swelling.

Primary Lymphedema

Secondary Lymphedema

Other Causes

Can also result from infection, injury, chronic venous insufficiency, obesity, or other conditions that affect lymphatic drainage.

Common Symptoms

  • Swelling in an arm, leg, or other body part
  • A feeling of heaviness or tightness
  • Restricted range of motion
  • Recurring infections in the affected area
  • Hardening or thickening of the skin (fibrosis)
  • Aching or discomfort
  • Clothing, shoes, or jewelry feeling tighter on one side

Stages of Lymphedema

Stage 0 (Latent): The lymphatic system is impaired but no visible swelling is present. May feel heaviness or mild discomfort.

Stage I (Mild): Swelling occurs that may improve with elevation. Pressing the skin leaves an indent (pitting edema).

Stage II (Moderate): Swelling rarely improves with elevation alone. Tissue begins to harden (fibrosis). Consistent treatment is needed.

Stage III (Severe): Significant swelling with skin changes, hardening, and increased risk of infection. The limb may be very large. Ongoing management is essential.

How Pneumatic Compression Therapy Works

Pneumatic compression therapy is a clinically proven treatment for lymphedema that uses a specialized medical device to help move excess fluid out of swollen limbs. It's often used alongside other treatments like manual lymph drainage, compression garments, exercise, and skin care.

The Device

A pneumatic compression device (PCD) consists of two parts:

  • The pump unit: A small, portable controller that generates precisely calibrated air pressure
  • The garment: A sleeve, boot, or vest with multiple air chambers that wraps around the affected limb or body area

How It Works

The device inflates the garment's chambers in a sequential pattern ‚Äî starting at the farthest point (e.g., the hand or foot) and moving toward the body. This creates a gentle, wave-like compression that:

  • Pushes excess lymphatic fluid back toward the body's central circulation
  • Mimics the natural pumping action of a healthy lymphatic system
  • Softens fibrotic (hardened) tissue over time
  • Reduces swelling, pain, and heaviness
  • Improves overall limb function and comfort

What to Expect

Treatment sessions typically last 30‚Äì60 minutes and are done at home, 1‚Äì2 times daily. Most patients find the therapy comfortable and relaxing. You can use the device while reading, watching TV, or resting. Many patients notice a reduction in swelling and heaviness within the first few weeks of consistent use.

Clinical Evidence

Pneumatic compression therapy is supported by extensive clinical research and is recommended by multiple medical guidelines. It has been shown to:

  • Significantly reduce limb volume in lymphedema patients
  • Improve patient quality of life and mobility
  • Reduce the frequency of cellulitis (skin infections)
  • Complement manual lymph drainage and other therapies
